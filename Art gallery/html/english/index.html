The Last Supper

"The Last Supper" is a famous painting created by the Italian Renaissance master Leonardo da Vinci. The painting depicts the last supper that Jesus Christ had with his apostles before he was betrayed. The painting holds significant historical and religious importance and has become an iconic representation. Jesus is depicted at the center of the painting, seated at a table surrounded by the twelve apostles. Leonardo skillfully captured the diverse expressions on the faces of the apostles, capturing their reactions to Jesus' words that one of them would betray him. This dramatic scene is recognizable and captivating, drawing the attention of viewers and eliciting various interpretations. The painting was created using the fresco technique, which involves applying paint to a wet plaster surface. However, over time, the fresco has suffered damages and undergone restorations, so the original state of the painting is not entirely preserved today. "The Last Supper" has become a symbol of Christianity and is one of Leonardo da Vinci's most famous works. Its artistic value, emotional power, and mysterious atmosphere continue to intrigue and inspire viewers worldwide.

Persistence of Memory

Capturing the essence of surrealism, Salvador Dali's iconic painting 'Persistence of Memory' showcases a dreamlike world where time appears to melt away. With melting clocks draped over abstract landscapes, this enigmatic masterpiece challenges our perception of reality, inviting us to ponder the nature of time and memory.

Starry Night

"Starry Night" is one of the most famous and recognizable works of art by Dutch post-impressionist painter Vincent van Gogh. Painted in 1889, it depicts a serene night sky filled with swirling, vibrant stars, a crescent moon, and a small village at the bottom. Van Gogh's signature expressive brushstrokes create a sense of movement and energy in the sky, while the dark cypress tree in the foreground adds a contrasting element. The painting is known for its rich colors, particularly the deep blues and yellows that dominate the composition. "Starry Night" is not only a representation of a physical place but also an evocative portrayal of the artist's emotions and his unique perspective on the beauty of the natural world.
